4. Step Buttons & LEDs

16 step buttons with matching LEDs form the step sequencer grid.

Normal Mode

  • Press a step to toggle it on/off in the currently selected track
  • Lit LED = step is active (voice triggers on that beat)
  • During playback, the current step LED pulses bright as the sequence plays

Bass Line Mode (D1 only)

  • Short press — toggles step on/off (same as normal)
  • Hold 300 ms — enters note-select for that step. Turn the Pitch knob to set its note. Release to confirm
  • You can toggle other steps while holding one in note-select

5. Bass Line Mode

Bass line mode turns the kick drum into a chromatic bass synthesizer with per-step pitch control.

How to Use

  1. Activate: Hold the D1 Select button for 3 seconds. Display shows "BASS LINE MODE"
  2. Program steps: Press step buttons to toggle beats on/off (short press)
  3. Set pitch per step: Hold any step button for 300 ms, then turn the D1 Pitch knob. The display shows the step number and note name (e.g., "STEP 5 — C3")
  4. Release the step button to confirm the note
  5. Deactivate: Hold D1 Select for 3 seconds again
Tip: Each step defaults to A2. Notes range from A1 to A4 (36 semitones). Bass line notes are saved with your pattern.

7. Delay Effect

The delay is a beat-synced echo shared by all three voices. Each voice has its own send level (knobs 7, 12, 20).

Delay Time (Knob 24)

Snaps to the nearest musical division based on your current BPM:

DivisionNameDescription
1/32Thirty-second noteVery fast flutter
1/16Sixteenth noteTight rhythmic echo
1/8TEighth tripletSwing/shuffle feel
1/8Eighth noteClassic echo
1/4TQuarter tripletSyncopated bounce
1/8.Dotted eighthPing-pong feel
1/4Quarter noteStandard echo
1/4.Dotted quarterWide spacious echo
1/2Half noteLong throw
5/8Five eighthsPolyrhythmic feel
1/2.Dotted halfVery long trail

Delay Amount (Knob 31)

  • 0–2%: Delay is completely off (dead band)
  • 2–25%: Volume ramps up to full
  • 25–100%: Volume stays at peak; feedback amount continues to increase
Tip: Muting a voice (volume to zero) does not cut its delay tail. Delay sends are independent of voice volumes.

Master effects chain:

  • Master LPF (knob 26) — Lowpass filter on the entire mix
  • Master Wavefolder (knobs 25 + 30) — Adds harmonics/distortion to the full mix
  • Delay (knobs 24 + 31) — Beat-synced echo with feedback, mixed into output

9. External Clock Sync

Connect an external clock pulse to sync the sequencer with other gear.

Getting Started

  1. Connect a clock source to the clock input
  2. Press Play — the machine waits for incoming pulses
  3. Once a steady clock is detected (two consistent pulses), it locks in and starts running
  4. The display shows "EXT" and the detected BPM

PPQN (Pulses Per Quarter Note)

Set PPQN to match your clock source. Hold the Slot button for 3 seconds, turn the BPM knob to select, release to confirm.

PPQNUse When
1Clock sends 1 pulse per beat — machine subdivides into 4 steps
2Clock sends 2 pulses per beat — machine subdivides into 2 steps
4Clock sends 4 pulses per beat — 1:1 step mapping
24Standard MIDI clock (24 ppqn)
48High-resolution clock
96Very high-resolution clock
Tip: If the external clock stops for more than 2 seconds, the machine returns to internal clock mode. PPQN is remembered across power cycles.

10. Save & Load

10 memory slots (0–9) store your patterns. Each slot saves:

  • D1 kick pattern (16 steps)
  • D2 snare/clap pattern (16 steps)
  • D3 hi-hat pattern (16 steps)
  • Bass line notes (pitch per step)

How to Save & Load

ActionHow
Choose a slotPress the Slot button to cycle through slots 0–9
SavePress Save — display shows "PATTERN SAVED"
LoadPress Load — display shows "PATTERN LOADED"
Tip: You can save while the sequence is playing. Knob positions are not saved — only the step patterns and bass line notes.
